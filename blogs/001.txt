Title: The Weight of Copper
Date: 2024-11-08
Type: poetry

---

I learned about resistance in a classroom,
how every wire fights the current,
how the world charges a price
for every electron that passes through.

No one told me
the same law applies
to people.

The greater the resistance,
the more heat you generate —
and I have been running so hot
for so long
that my insulation is wearing thin.

I sat with an ohmmeter once,
pressing its probes to a length of copper wire,
watching the display blink: 0.3 Ω.
Barely a fight.
Just smooth and unbroken passage.

I have never been copper.
I am something with a much higher resistance —
the kind that wastes energy as heat,
the kind that glows red before it breaks.

But perhaps that is something too.
To glow.
Even briefly.
Even alone.
